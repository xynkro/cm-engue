The book · published November 2025

This is not a self-help book.
This is a confession.

Written as a letter to his younger sister, 25 Things I Wished I Knew at 25 lays bare the lessons C.M Engue learned the expensive way — wasting $300,000 on things that didn't matter, choosing work over family until it was almost too late, and treating his body like it would run forever until it suddenly didn't.

It's what your older brother would tell you — if he loved you enough to be brutal.

About the author

The notes were never meant to be a book.

C.M Engue writes about the things most people take a decade to figure out.

His first book started as a private notes file — lessons about money, work, and relationships he kept wishing someone had told him earlier. When his sister turned twenty-five, he wrote them down for her. The book that resulted is direct, personal, and built for people still working out what they actually want from their lives.

He lives and works in Singapore.

— C.M E.
